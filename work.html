Services

Choose the lane that matches the current work.

Assistant workflow audit Map what your assistants are doing now, where context is leaking, where sessions restart, and where human approval needs to be clearer.

Good for: Teams that already use AI and need an evidence-based route from messy output to repeatable work.

Typical output: A workflow map, boundary notes, handoff risks, and the next practical improvement path.

Private continuity system Turn scattered notes, repo state, operating rules, and handoffs into a durable assistant-ready workspace.

Good for: High-context work that loses time when every assistant session starts over.

Typical output: A local continuity map, memory boundaries, approval gates, and restartable handoff structure.

Product and support architecture Design knowledge base, support bot, dashboard, rate-limit, and member-flow boundaries before providers are connected.

Good for: Product or support teams planning help centers, AI support lanes, or operator dashboards.

Typical output: A service map, support taxonomy, provider gates, escalation model, and launch constraints.

Launch readiness review Prepare public pages, lead paths, payment gates, measurement posture, and rollback checks before release.

Good for: Sites, services, and public workflows that need promise control before more visibility.

Typical output: A readiness checklist, claim review, provider boundary check, and rollback notes.

Not a fit

Provider gates

Before leads or money move.

  1. Lead destination selected: Tally hosted form. Embeds, CRM routing, and webhooks remain separate gates.
  2. First paid service selected: Stripe-hosted paid discovery. Embeds, subscriptions, invoices, and suite checkout remain separate gates.
  3. Approve privacy notice, retention, refunds, receipts, taxes, and support policy.
  4. Verify notification delivery, spam protection, and failure-state copy before scaling lead capture.
  5. Verify provider settings and rollback before deployment.
