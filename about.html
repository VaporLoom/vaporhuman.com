About

A human layer for assistant systems.

VaporHuman is for the parts of AI work that need clarity, consent, memory, and handoff discipline.

It is not trying to make an assistant sound more human. It is trying to keep the actual human visible.

Why this exists

AI work needs a readable contract.

Assistants can be useful, but usefulness is not the same as trust. A system should make it easy to see what it is doing, what it is remembering, what it is guessing, and what still needs human judgment.

VaporHuman gives that work a name and a public place to explain the posture before making bigger claims about products, services, accounts, or availability.

Focus areas

What VaporHuman pays attention to

Boundaries

Memory

Useful continuity without pretending notes, summaries, or retrieval are perfect.

Handoffs

Clear transitions between humans, agents, tools, repos, and future replacements.

Plain language

Explanations that normal people can challenge, correct, and improve.

Current state

Early, static public output, and deliberately modest.

This site is currently a trust surface with a hosted work-intake link. It also links to a Stripe-hosted paid discovery session. It does not run analytics, provide professional advice, process card data directly, or announce guaranteed service availability.

That restraint is intentional. The first job is to explain the trust boundary clearly before adding anything more powerful.

VaporHuman designs private AI continuity systems. Site copy, page structure, availability, and future features may change as the product lane develops.

This site does not provide medical, legal, financial, employment, identity, emergency, or professional advice. Assistant output should support human judgment, not replace it.

The work-intake path uses a hosted Tally form, and paid discovery uses a hosted Stripe Payment Link. This site does not embed forms, login, checkout, analytics, tracking scripts, public email address, donation flow, subscriptions, or live assistant service.
