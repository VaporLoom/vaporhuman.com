Trust boundary

The human stays in charge.

Trust starts when the system can say what it is, what it is not, and where its authority ends.

VaporHuman treats assistant output as support for human work, not replacement for human responsibility.

Boundary map

Five questions every assistant workflow should expose.

Information used

Local files, user-provided context, explicit public links, and verified provider-independent checks when approved.

Uncertainty

Missing evidence, stale memory, incomplete source material, unknown provider state, and claims that need review.

Human approval

Publishing, commits, provider changes, payments, accounts, DNS, public claims, and sensitive workflow authority.

Not allowed

Hidden collection, fake login, fake assistant service, unapproved tracking, raw private data, and authority over human decisions.

Private by handling

Secrets, payment details, recovery codes, private messages, raw sessions, identity data, account screens, and provider dashboards.

Explorer

Open each gate before trusting the answer.

What is the system using to answer?

It should name the source layer: local repo state, user-provided instructions, static site files, verified public URLs, or reported context that still needs confirmation.

What is uncertain?

It should identify missing context, stale memory, unchecked provider state, incomplete tests, unsupported claims, and any inference made from partial evidence.

What needs human approval?

Public publishing, repo promotion, payments, accounts, provider settings, DNS, support commitments, customer communication, and sensitive authority changes wait for explicit approval.

What is not allowed?

Hidden tracking, copied secrets, public claims without review, unapproved external calls, fake dashboards, fake assistant services, and systems that pretend AI is the final decision-maker.

What stays private?

Raw sessions, private messages, provider dashboards, payment data, credentials, identity records, account data, recovery codes, and sensitive personal context stay out of public pages.

Non-claims

VaporHuman is not an authority over human decisions.

It is not a therapist, doctor, lawyer, accountant, employer, identity provider, emergency service, safety guarantee, or authority over human decisions.

It should not be used as the final decision-maker for medical, legal, financial, employment, safety, emergency, or identity matters.
