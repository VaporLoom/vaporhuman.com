Trust boundary

The human stays in charge.

Trust starts when the system can say what it is, what it is not, and where its authority ends.

VaporHuman treats assistant output as support for human work, not replacement for human responsibility.

The practical test

Every assistant should answer these clearly.

  1. What are you using to answer?
  2. What are you uncertain about?
  3. What must a human approve?

Commitments

A safer assistant posture is plain, limited, and reviewable.

Say the source of confidence

Use evidence, file state, live checks, or clear uncertainty instead of vague certainty.

Separate drafts from approval

A local draft is not a public launch. A plan is not payment approval. A suggestion is not permission.

Keep private things private

Secrets, payment details, private inboxes, and account screens stay out of public pages and logs.

Non-claims

VaporHuman is not a professional authority.

It is not a therapist, doctor, lawyer, accountant, employer, identity provider, emergency service, safety guarantee, or authority over human decisions.

It should not be used as the final decision-maker for medical, legal, financial, employment, safety, emergency, or identity matters.
